remains valid until the stars come into contact. SHow that the inspiral time from the current orbital separation $a_0$ for a circular binary orbit is given by:
\begin{equation}
    \tau_{insp} = \frac{5}{256}\frac{c^5}{G^3}\frac{a_0^4}{M^2 \mu}
\end{equation}
Where $M$ is the total and $\mu$ the reduced mass. 
}
We get a reduced equation when we consider zero eccentricity:
\begin{equation}
    \odv{a}{t} = -\frac{64G^3m_1m_2M}{5a^3c^5}
\end{equation}
Let's absorb $m_1m_2 = \mu M$:
\begin{equation}
    \odv{a}{t} = -\frac{64G^3\mu M^2}{5a^3c^5}
\end{equation}
We can then treat this as a separable equation to recover the inspiral time (from when we go from $a_0\rightarrow 0$):
\begin{equation}
    a^3\d a = -\frac{64G^3\mu M^2}{5c^5}\d t
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
    \int_{a_0}^{0}a^3 \d a = \int_{0}^{\tau_{insp}}- \frac{64G^3\mu M^2}{5c^5}\d t
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
    -\frac{a_0^4}{4} = -\frac{64G^3\mu M^2}{5c^5} \tau_{insp}
\end{equation}
Where we finally have:
\begin{equation}
    \tau_{insp} = \frac{5}{256}\frac{c^5}{G^3}\frac{a_0^4}{M^2\mu}
\end{equation}
