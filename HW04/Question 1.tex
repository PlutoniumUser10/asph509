\kafkasection{the dispersion relation for electromagnetic waves is given by
\begin{equation}
    \omega^2 = \frac{c^2k^2}{\epsilon}
\end{equation}
Where $\epsilon$ is the dielectric constant of the medium through which the wave propagates. For many astrophysical plasmas, the permittivity can be approximated as $\epsilon \approx 1 - \omega_p^2/\omega^2$, where 
\begin{equation}
    \omega_p^2 = 4\pi n_e \frac{e^2}{m_e}
\end{equation}
is the plasma frequency. Work out the group velocity $v_{group} = \pdv{\omega}{k}$ 
and the travel time.
\begin{equation}
    t_{travel} = \frac{d}{c^2} + \frac{2\pi e^2}{m_e \omega^2}DM
\end{equation}
Where $d/c$ is the travel time at very high frequencies, and DM is the dispersion measure defined as:
\begin{equation}
    DM = \int_{0}^{d} n_e \d s
\end{equation}
