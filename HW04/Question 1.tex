\begin{equation}
    \omega^2 = \frac{c^2k^2}{\epsilon}
\end{equation}
Where $\epsilon$ is the dielectric constant of the medium through which the wave propagates. For many astrophysical plasmas, the permittivity can be approximated as $\epsilon \approx 1 - \omega_p^2/\omega^2$, where 
\begin{equation}
    \omega_p^2 = 4\pi n_e \frac{e^2}{m_e}
\end{equation}
is the plasma frequency. Work out the group velocity $v_{group} = \pdv{\omega}{k}$ 
and the travel time.
\begin{equation}
    t_{travel} = \frac{d}{c} + \frac{2\pi e^2}{m_e \omega^2}DM
\end{equation}
Where $d/c$ is the travel time at very high frequencies, and DM is the dispersion measure defined as:
\begin{equation}
    DM = \int_{0}^{d} n_e \d s
\end{equation}

Let's input $\epsilon$ into the expression for the dispersion relation:
\begin{equation}
    \omega^2 = \frac{c^2k^2}{1 - \omega_p^2/\omega^2}
\end{equation}
Let's put this in terms of $k$ to take advantage of this random ahh $1- \omega_p^2/\omega^2$
\begin{equation}
    k = \frac{\omega}{c}\sqrt{1-\omega_p^2/\omega^2}
\end{equation}
The partial w.r.t $\omega$ is then:
\begin{align}
    \pdv{k}{\omega} &= \frac{1}{c}\sqrt{1-\omega_p^2/\omega^2}+ \frac{\omega}{2c}\ab(1-\omega_p^2/\omega^2)^{-1/2}\ab(\frac{-2\omega_p^2}{\omega^{3}})\\
    &= \frac{1}{c}\ab(1-\omega_p^2\omega^2)^{-1/2}
\end{align}
The group velocity given by the following partial is then:
\begin{equation}
    v_{group} = \ab(\pdv{k}{\omega})^{-1} = c\sqrt{1-\omega_p^2/\omega^2}
\end{equation}
The travel time is then:
\begin{align}
    t_{travel} &= \int_{0}^{d}\frac{1}{v_{group}}\d s\\
    &= \int_{0}^{d}\frac{1}{c\sqrt{1-\omega_p^2/\omega^2}}\d s\\
    &= \int_{0}^{d}\frac{1}{c}\ab(1-\omega_p^2/\omega^2)^{-1/2}\d s\\
    &= \int_{0}^{d}\frac{1}{c}\ab(1-\frac{4\pi n_e e^2}{m_e\omega^2})^{-1/2}\d s\\
    &= \int_{0}^{d}\frac{1}{c}\ab(1+\frac{2\pi n_e e^2}{m_e\omega^2})\d s
\end{align}
Where we used the binomial expansion to get rid of the square root.
The itegral is then:
\begin{equation}
    \frac{d}{c}+\frac{2\pi e^2}{c m_e\omega^2}\int_{0}^{d}n_e \d s
\end{equation}
Where we finally have:
\begin{equation}
    t_{travel} = \frac{d}{c}+\frac{2\pi e^2}{c m_e\omega^2}DM
\end{equation}
