\kafkasection{The growth of black hole can be described by
\begin{equation}
    \odv{M}{t} = \frac{L}{c^2\eta}
\end{equation}
Show that:
\begin{equation}
    t_{growth} = \frac{L_{edd}\sigma_T c\eta}{4\pi Gm_p L}
\end{equation}
}
Let's treat this differential equation as separable:
\begin{equation}
    \d M = \frac{L}{c^2\eta} \d t
\end{equation}
We'll integrate from $0\rightarrow M$ and $0\rightarrow t_{growth}$. This gives:
\begin{equation}
    M = \frac{L}{c^2\eta}t_{growth}
\end{equation}
Now let's substitute this mass with the one from the eddington luminosity as we consider an accreting object.
\begin{equation}
    M = \frac{L_{Edd}\sigma_T}{4\pi G m_p c} = \frac{L}{c^2\eta}t_{growth}
\end{equation}
Now let's rearrange for the time:
\begin{equation}
    t_{growth} = \frac{L_{edd}\sigma_T c\eta}{4\pi Gm_p L}
\end{equation}
