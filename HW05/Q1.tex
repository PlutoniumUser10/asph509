\kafkasection{Assume that a typical GRB emits its gamma rays into two cones of half-openin angle $\theta_j = \qty{4}{\degree}$ as shown in figure 7.8. Adopt a value of two observed bursts per day to obtain a lower limit on the true GRB rate.}
If we assume that GRBs are random in the direction the cone points. We can divide out the solid angle made from the 2 cones from $4\pi$ to get a factor on our 2 rates per day.
\begin{equation}
    \theta_j = \qty{4}{degree} \rightarrow \ohm = 4\pi(1-\cos\theta_j) = 0.0306110472
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
    \frac{4\pi}{0.0306110472} = 410.51
\end{equation}
So then our rate is $2\cdot410.51 = 821$ GRB per day.
